Why Florida Sellers Choose Us

Florida land is hard to sell the traditional way.

Vacant lots in Lee, Charlotte, and Brevard counties can sit on the MLS for 12–24 months. Most Florida land buyers need bank financing — and banks barely lend on raw land. Here's what you're up against.

⏳

Florida land sits for 12–24 months on the MLS

Vacant lots in Lee and Charlotte counties average 400+ days on market. Most listings expire with zero offers while you keep paying taxes.

🏦

Banks won't finance Florida vacant land

Lenders view raw Florida parcels as high-risk speculative assets. Most retail buyers can't get approved — your deal falls apart after months of waiting.

🐢

Environmental issues stall Florida deals

Gopher tortoise habitat, scrub jay presence, wetland setbacks — Florida's environmental regulations kill deals that are months in the making. We already know how to navigate them.

💸

Property taxes keep coming while the land sits

Lee County property taxes on a 1-acre lot average $400–$900/year. Every year you hold, you're paying for an asset doing nothing for you.

Common Questions

Florida land sellers ask us this.

Do you buy land in Lee County and Charlotte County Florida?
−
Yes. Lee County and Charlotte County are two of our most active Florida markets. We buy vacant lots in Cape Coral, Lehigh Acres, LaBelle, Port Charlotte, Englewood, Rotonda, and surrounding areas. If your parcel is in Southwest Florida, we want to hear from you.
Will you buy Florida land with gopher tortoise habitat or environmental issues?
+
Yes. We are experienced buyers of Florida land with environmental constraints, including parcels with gopher tortoise habitat, scrub jay presence, wetland setbacks, or failed perc tests. These factors affect our offer amount but do not disqualify your land from consideration.
How fast can you close on Florida land?
+
We deliver a written cash offer within 48 hours and close through a Florida-licensed title company in 14 to 21 days. Most Florida sellers have cash in their account within three weeks of first contact.
Do you buy landlocked or flood zone land in Florida?
+
Yes. We evaluate landlocked parcels and properties in flood zones AE, X, and X500. Florida flood zone designation affects our offer calculation but does not prevent us from making an offer. We have closed on difficult-access Florida land many times.
I inherited land in Florida and don't know what to do — can you help?
+
Absolutely. Inherited land is one of the most common situations we work with in Florida. Whether the estate is settled or still in probate, whether you have the deed or not — send us the parcel details and our team will guide you through the process.
Do you buy Florida land with back property taxes?
+
Yes. We buy tax-delinquent land in Florida regularly. In most cases we absorb the outstanding taxes as part of the deal so you do not need to bring money to closing. The number we offer is what you receive — nothing deducted.
